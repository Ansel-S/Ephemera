# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Ephemera is a tool built around one idea: that your attention is finite and worth protecting. We bring that same intentionality to how we interact with each other. Everyone who participates in this project — whether by filing a bug, suggesting a feature, or opening a pull request — is expected to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone.

This applies regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**Behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Being kind and respectful in all communications
- Welcoming newcomers and people of all experience levels
- Giving constructive, actionable feedback
- Accepting feedback gracefully
- Focusing on the problem, not the person

**Behavior that is not acceptable:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Insulting, condescending, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing anyone's private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any related communications. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub issue or contacting the maintainer directly. All reports will be reviewed promptly and treated with discretion.

The maintainer reserves the right to close issues, reject pull requests, or block accounts that violate this Code of Conduct.
